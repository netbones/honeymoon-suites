Historical Context

The 1990s Cape Town Scene

The Honeymoon Suites emerged during a pivotal time in South African music history. Cape Town was becoming "the new crucible of white South African rock," as described by the Mail & Guardian in 1998.

The band's theatrical approach and glamrock aesthetic stood out in a scene that was evolving beyond traditional rock boundaries. Their performances at venues like The Magnet, The Lounge, and the "kitsch" Graceland became legendary.

The Klein Karoo Festival residency at the old casino behind a butchery became the stuff of legend, with fans dancing on tables and the band changing costumes every night.
